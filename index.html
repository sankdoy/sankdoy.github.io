About Me

I'm a Sound Design student at the University of Hertfordshire with a passion for creating immersive audio experiences for film, TV, and games.

I particularly enjoy working with Max/MSP for custom synthesis and procedural audio, FMOD for adaptive music systems, and integrating sound into Unity projects. I'm excited about the role AI will play in the music and audio industries - from streamlining workflows to opening new creative possibilities - and I'm committed to staying current with emerging tools and innovations. I'm pro-AI and believe it will enhance, rather than replace, human creativity in sound design.

My experience includes recording and mixing live bands (both on-location and post-production for video sync), as well as building interactive audio demos and game soundscapes.

Selected Projects

Interactive Synthesizer (Max/MSP)

A browser-playable custom synthesizer built in Max/MSP and exported via RNBO. Features real-time parameter control for experimentation with timbre, modulation, and effects.

Procedural Music Generator (Max/MSP)

A generative music system in Max/MSP that creates random, evolving compositions based on user-defined rules and probability - demonstrating algorithmic audio techniques.

Adaptive Music Demo (FMOD)

An interactive FMOD project inspired by the sound design of Dune, with real-time parameter control (e.g., intensity/health sliders) that trigger seamless music transitions and layer changes.

Horror Game Soundscape (Unity)

An atmospheric sound design implementation in Unity for a horror game section, including layered ambiences, Foley, and dynamic triggers for tension and immersion.

Live Band Recording & Mixing
